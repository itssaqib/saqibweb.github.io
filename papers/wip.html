Terrorism and Voting

This paper examines the political consequences of terrorism in Pakistan. We exploit the death of Osama bin Laden as an instrumental variable for terrorist attacks and use quasi-random variation between successful and failed attacks across regions. Using candidate- and constituency-level electoral data, we find that successful terrorist attacks significantly increase the vote share of left-wing populist and independent candidates, while reducing support for right-wing parties. These findings suggest how terrorism can reshape the political landscape by shifting voter preferences toward populist alternatives.
